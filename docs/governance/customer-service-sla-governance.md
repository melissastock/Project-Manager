# Customer Service SLA Governance

## Purpose

Define minimum customer support service levels and escalation governance for customer-facing projects.

This ensures:
- response expectations are explicit
- escalations are handled consistently
- unresolved issues do not linger without ownership
- customer trust is protected during incidents

---

## Applicability

This policy is mandatory for any project that is:
- customer-facing
- client-delivery critical
- responsible for issue intake, triage, or resolution workflows

---

## Required Documents

---

## Minimum SLA Standards

- Define support tiers (P0, P1, P2, P3)
- Define first-response targets for each tier
- Define status-update cadence for active incidents
- Define target resolution windows by tier
- Define business hours and after-hours coverage expectations

---

## Governance Rules

- Assign a named support owner
- Track SLA exceptions with root-cause notes
- Review SLA performance at least monthly
- Publish escalation contacts and backup approvers
- Do not close customer-impacting incidents without documented outcome

---

## Staffing And Training Readiness

Begin CS staffing and training when any of the following are true:
- production launch date is committed
- pilot or beta includes external customers
- forecasted ticket volume exceeds ad hoc handling capacity
- P0/P1 incidents could occur without dedicated response coverage

Minimum readiness requirements before customer launch:
- primary support owner and backup are named
- escalation owners are assigned for each severity level
- training on product workflows and incident response is completed
- support runbook walkthrough is completed
- initial knowledge base and macros are published

Training governance:
- complete role-based onboarding before on-call eligibility
- run at least one escalation simulation per quarter
- review SLA misses and update training content monthly

---

## Enforcement

A customer-facing project is NOT operationally onboarded until:
- SLA document exists
- escalation paths are documented
- support owner and backup are assigned
- response and resolution targets are approved
- staffing/training readiness is documented for launch

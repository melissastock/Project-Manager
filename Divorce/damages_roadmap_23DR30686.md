# Damages Roadmap

Case No. `2023DR30686`

This roadmap is a working file for counsel. It separates:

- damages that are cleanly provable now,
- damages that look supportable with one more document set,

It is designed to answer one practical question:

**Why the current damages picture is materially larger than the bare `$17,496.30` arrears figure.**

### What Bucket A Means

Bucket A does **not** mean every number is automatically recoverable in full.

It means the file already proves:

- a core contempt amount of at least `$17,496.30` before late fees,
- documented displacement housing,
- documented post-sale debt paydown,
- and documented family-loan repayment outflows.

That is enough to safely argue that the financial damage picture is materially larger than the arrears number alone.

For filing accuracy, the motion should distinguish between:

- amounts clearly still sought as judgment now, and
- amounts that prove admitted noncompliance and damage even if later partially absorbed through closing reconciliation.

## Best Current Framing

The safest damages argument right now is:

1. The Court should enter judgment on the clean contempt figures first:
   - `$2,929.32` November reimbursement
   - plus any proven late fees
   - and any net mortgage amount still shown to remain unpaid after closing reconciliation
2. The file already shows that Melissa's financial harm was not limited to those arrears.
3. By December 2023, the financial collapse was already underway:
   - family-loan support was being used for attorney fees, rent, deposits, mortgage obligations, roof expense, and rental fixes;
   - Melissa was seeking emergency financial stabilization and trying to avoid foreclosure;
   - and ordinary recovery was no longer intact.
4. The Court should permit supplementation of consequential damages by exhibit chart and underlying statements.

Separate SBA objective:

- The SBA issue should not be folded into the clean arrears number.
- The strongest current ask is production plus preservation:
  - get the lender-side file,
  - preserve a Rule `16.2` or similar reallocation / release request,
  - and seek an end to Melissa's remaining SBA obligation only after the fuller proof set is assembled.

## Bottom Line

The current file supports a strong position that the case is **not** a `$17,496.30 and done` enforcement motion.

## Purpose Of This Handoff

This document is a litigation-safer rewrite of the current post-divorce motion package. It is meant to preserve the strongest documented facts, clearly identify where the proof line is indirect, and flag where additional records are still needed before a filing should make a definitive factual assertion.

This handoff is designed to support:

1. A post-decree contempt / enforcement motion.
2. A contractual indemnification / attorney-fee claim under the Separation Agreement.
3. A possible C.R.C.P. 16.2 reallocation motion regarding the SBA loan, if counsel concludes the current and obtainable evidence supports it.

This draft is intentionally careful about labels:

- `Documented Fact`: Supported directly by a court record, bank record, statement, contract, closing record, or a party admission.
- `Contemporaneous Statement`: Supported by Melissa's email, text, or statement made during the events at issue. Useful and often important, but not always a substitute for third-party proof.
- `Inference / Needs Additional Proof`: A fair working conclusion or theory, but not yet something that should be stated as conclusively proven without more records.

Where numbers remain unresolved, they should stay unresolved in any filed pleading until the underlying document is attached or specifically cited.

---

## Core Framing For Drafting

This case is strongest when framed as a document-based enforcement and damages case, not as a generalized divorce grievance. The motion should focus on:

1. The Separation Agreement became a court-enforceable order when incorporated into the decree.
2. Respondent failed to perform multiple specific obligations required by that agreement.
3. Petitioner continued performing her own obligations and continued paying expenses Respondent was supposed to pay.
4. Those failures caused direct and consequential financial harm.
5. The current record strongly supports an SBA misrepresentation theory, but the exact use of proceeds still needs cleaner lender-side or vendor-side proof before being stated as fully established fact.

### Important Causation Note

Even where a debt payment is documented, the filing should distinguish:

- the existence of the debt,
- the fact that Petitioner paid it from sale proceeds,
- and the further claim that the debt was caused by Respondent's noncompliance.

That third step often requires statement-level tracing or at least a clean narrative tie to the relevant month and obligation.

## Part 6: Drafting Instructions For Counsel Or AI Drafting

### Use This Tone

- restrained;
- document-led;
- specific;
- not neutral in the sense of flattening proven noncompliance;
- but also not categorical beyond what the record currently proves.

### Avoid These Overstatements

Do not write:

- "all factual claims are fully proven by uploaded documents";
- "the SBA loan definitely did not pay credit cards and definitely went into real estate" unless additional proof is attached;
- "Respondent contributed nothing" unless that is tied to an actual evidentiary showing;
- "bankruptcy was caused solely by Respondent" unless supported by expert or a much tighter proof chain;
- "Respondent walked away without paying arrears" unless the closing statement confirms that.

### Preferred Motion Language Examples

Use:

- "The current record shows..."
- "Respondent admitted in writing..."
- "Melissa contemporaneously reported..."
- "The presently reviewed file does not contain proof that..."
- "This supports an inference that..."
- "This issue warrants reallocation discovery / further production..."

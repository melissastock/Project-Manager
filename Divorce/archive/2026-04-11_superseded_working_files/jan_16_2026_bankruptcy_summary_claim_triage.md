# January 16, 2026 Summary Claim Triage

## Purpose

This memo triages the claims in Melissa's January 16, 2026 summary email into three categories:

- `Document-backed now`
- `Supportable with careful wording`
- `Do not use yet without more proof`

The goal is not to weaken the story. The goal is to separate the parts that are already strong from the parts that need tightening or more records before they should appear in a lawyer memo, motion, declaration, or bankruptcy narrative.

## Best Overall Takeaway

The January 16 summary is strongest when used as:

- a high-level explanatory narrative for counsel,
- a roadmap for document collection,
- and a chronology of what Melissa understood had happened.

It is weakest when used as if every sentence is already independently proven by lender files, bank statements, tax records, or court-ordered accounting.

## 2. Supportable With Careful Wording

These points likely belong in the story, but they should be phrased more carefully than they were in the January 16 email.

### Sole provider / sole financial support

- Original version:
  - `From 2015 through 2024, I was the sole financial provider in the household.`
- Safer version:
  - `The current file strongly supports that Melissa was the primary borrower, primary mortgage obligor, and principal financial support for the household and real estate, while Jesse's financial contribution was intermittent and materially lower.`
- Why:
  - The mortgage/asset records strongly support primary responsibility.
  - `Sole provider` is broader than the current document set cleanly proves across the entire 2015-2024 period.

### Three homes purchased using Melissa's income, assets, and retirement funds

- Original version:
  - `I purchased three homes using my income, assets, and retirement funds, and was the only borrower on all mortgages.`
- Safer version:
  - `The current file supports that Melissa was the only borrower on the mortgages and that she used her own income and assets, including retirement-related funds, to support acquisition and carrying costs.`
- Why:
  - `Only borrower on all mortgages` appears supportable.
  - The broader acquisition-funding statement should remain tethered to the actual closing and funding documents.

### Jesse treated rental management as his job and claimed entitlement to rental earnings

- Original version:
  - `He was added to titles and considered renting and managing tenants and Airbnb units his “job,” claiming entitlement to rental earnings and ownership benefits...`
- Safer version:
  - `The file supports that Jesse treated rental and Airbnb management as his principal role and asserted control over rental and Airbnb income while Melissa remained the primary mortgage obligor.`
- Why:
  - The income-control point is strong.
  - `Entitlement to ownership benefits` is directionally right but should be tied to specific communications if used aggressively.

### Refinance under duress

- Original version:
  - `These circumstances directly forced me to refinance all three properties in 2020.`
- Safer version:
  - `Melissa's current account is that the 2020 refinances were undertaken because the household financial structure had become unsustainable and Jesse was not contributing consistently to shared obligations.`
- Why:
  - The coercion and unsustainability theory may be true, but the refinance causation chain needs the actual 2020 refinance records before being stated as established fact.

### SBA misrepresentation theory

- Original version:
  - `During mediation, he misrepresented the loan as having been used for my credit cards...`
- Safer version:
  - `The current file supports a serious factual dispute over the SBA loan. Melissa consistently states she was told the loan was tied to her credit cards, but the present records do not yet contain the full lender-side SBA file needed to prove origination purpose and use of proceeds conclusively.`
- Why:
  - This is a key theory, but still needs lender documents and tracing.

### Outside rent and legal fees

- Original version:
  - `This forced me to take a $2,500 rental of my own while simultaneously paying my lawyer $2,500 per month until he finally signed the settlement.`
- Safer version:
  - `The current file strongly supports that Melissa incurred outside housing costs during the divorce and was carrying substantial legal fees at the same time. The exact month-by-month rent amount after December 2023 still needs additional documentation.`
- Why:
  - The outside-housing theory is strong.

### Debt and insolvency outcome

- Original version:
  - `By the time the homes were sold in 2024, I had incurred approximately $125,000 in debt (family loans and credit cards), a $75,000 tax liability, and significant legal fees.`
- Safer version:
  - `Melissa's current position is that by the time the homes were sold she had incurred very substantial family-loan, credit-card, tax, and legal-fee burdens. The exact totals should be tied to transfer records, card statements, tax records, and fee records before use as fixed numbers.`
- Why:
  - The harm theory is plausible and likely right.
  - The exact numbers still need record support.

### Unequal sale outcome

- Original version:
  - `He received approximately $250,000 and paid no debts; I was left insolvent.`
- Safer version:
  - `The current closing records support that Jesse received substantial sale proceeds, while Melissa used sale proceeds to satisfy large existing obligations. The record should be reconciled carefully before stating exact net distributions or claiming that Jesse paid no debt at all.`
- Why:
  - We have sale numbers, but broad `paid no debts` wording is too absolute without full tracing.

### `Late 2024 – Tax liability of $75,000 due to timing of forced asset liquidation`

- Do not use as a fixed damages statement unless supported by tax returns, tax notices, or CPA workpapers.

### `Bankruptcy is the final step in a decade-long process of being financially hollowed out`

- Fine as personal narrative or attorney background.
